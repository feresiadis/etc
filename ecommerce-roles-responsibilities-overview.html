Brief Overview of Roles and Responsibilities in E-Commerce

In an e-commerce context, it is essential to clearly delineate the different areas of expertise and their respective responsibilities to ensure effective management and operation.

Understanding these distinct roles and responsibilities ensures a well-functioning e-commerce operation that effectively serves both the store owner and the client. Clear delineation of duties facilitates better communication and collaboration among teams.

1. Graphic Design Marketing / Creative Team (source: Journal of Marketing Theory and Practice)

Graphic design focuses on the visual aspects of the e-commerce website. Responsibilities include:

  • Creating a cohesive brand identity through logos, color schemes, and typography.

  • Designing user interfaces (UI) that enhance user experience (UX).

  • Developing promotional materials and product images that align with the overall aesthetic and functionality of the website.

2. Web Development IT Department / Web Development Team (source: Journal of the Association for Information Systems)

Web development involves building the technical framework of the e-commerce site. Key responsibilities include:

  • Coding the front-end and back-end components of the website using languages such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP.

  • Implementing functionalities such as shopping carts, payment gateways, and product databases.

  • Ensuring the website is responsive, secure, and optimized for performance and search engines (SEO).

3. E-Commerce Application Management E-Commerce Operations, often overseen by IT Department (source: Chaffey’s Digital Business and E-Commerce Management)

Managing the e-commerce application encompasses the oversight of software functionalities. Responsibilities include:

  • Configuring and maintaining the e-commerce platform (e.g., Magento, WooCommerce) to ensure it meets the store's operational needs.

  • Managing product listings, including categorization, inventory tracking, and price updates.

  • Implementing analytics tools to monitor sales performance, customer behavior, and site traffic.

4. Website Administration and Management IT Department / Content Management Team, with input from Marketing (source: Journal of Digital Media Management)

Administrating and managing the website entails the ongoing maintenance and operational oversight. Responsibilities include:

  • Regularly updating content, products, and promotions to keep the site relevant and engaging.

  • Monitoring site performance and user feedback to make informed decisions on improvements.

  • Implementing security measures to protect customer data and prevent breaches.

5. Using the Website as the Store Owner Business Operations / Sales Team (source: eMarketing The Essential Guide to Marketing in a Digital World)

As the store owner, responsibilities include:

  • Utilizing the e-commerce platform to manage daily operations, including order processing and customer service.

  • Analyzing sales data and customer insights to inform business decisions and marketing strategies.

  • Overseeing inventory management and supply chain logistics to ensure product availability.

6. Using the Website as the Client Customer Care / User Experience (UX) Team (source: Brand Experience: What Is It? How Is It Measured? Does It Affect Loyalty?)

From the client's perspective, the focus is on usability and experience. Responsibilities include:

  • Navigating the site to find products and information efficiently.

  • Engaging with customer service for support and inquiries.

  • Completing transactions and providing feedback to improve the overall shopping experience.

Brief evaluation of e-commerce needs

The following questions will help gather essential information about the client's needs and facilitate the design of a tailored e-commerce solution.

1. Membership Requirements

Are you considering implementing a membership system for your customers? If so, what benefits or features do you envision offering to members?

2. Differentiated Pricing

Will you require differentiated pricing structures for members versus non-members? How do you anticipate managing these pricing tiers?

3. Product Complexity

Do your products involve complex attributes or configurations beyond standard categories, such as clothing? Could you provide examples of the types of attributes that are important for your offerings?

4. Brand Representation

Do you exclusively offer products from a single brand, or do you represent multiple brands? How does this influence your e-commerce strategy?

5. Business Model Clarification

Can you clarify your business model? Are you primarily a retail store, a wholesaler offering bulk products to retail and or individual customers, a representative of one or more brands, or do you encompass a combination of these roles?
